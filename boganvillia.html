Family;-)
Nyctaginaceae
Origin;-)
Southern Brazil
Description;-)
Bougainvillea California Gold is named for the stunning golden bracts that grow around the true flowers. It is a free flowing , vigerous, evergreen shrub or vine with clusters of warm golden yellow , papery bracets surrounding tiny creamy-white flowers. The flowers are borne on stems clad with short thorn and dull, wavy edged, ovate green leaves.They do not cling and may require tying for support.
Environment;-)
Bougainvillea are easy to grow and flower if grown under maximum light and stressed with dryness. Bring the soil to a state of visual dryness between watering. The best flowering occurs when the plants are brought to a slight wilt between waterings. When watering, thoroughly saturate the soil until a little water runs out of the bottom of the pot. Growing in a clay pot will help maintain a healthy root system.
Landscape Uses;-)
